Hard drives

  • Current status:
    • (see “Proposed Strategy” in the minutes of the meeting on 11th October)
    • pip has 3 × 500GB hard drives, and one “very nice” (~£260) 4TB enterprise (faster, more reliable, better / 5 year warranty) disk, donated at the Dinner (above). For reference, a desktop 4TB drive is £125; a slightly lower-end enterprise drive is ~£200.
    • The plan is to replace the 500GB disks as they fail until we have a full set of 4TB disks and can enlarge the RAID.
    • We cannot have any new large quotas until we enlarge the RAID.
  • Finances:
    • It was noted that the transition will be spread over “3 years or so”, and that the cost of disks is spread over the length of their warranty (5 years, for the disks we were considering).
    • We could do a donation drive (this would have to be the next committee)—it has been done before and has worked when we did it, though we try not to do it too often.
  • CUSU:
    • ... owes us a disk—we need to investigate whether they are happy to contribute towards a better disk (~£50), or are only willing to give us an equivalent disk back.
    • ... may be interested in our one 500GB spare that would otherwise go to waste.
  • Misc:
    • In an emergency we can go to RAID 1 (from RAID 10) by buying one more 4TB disk. We’d then be sorted for 5 years, though with worse performance.
  • Action (Malcolm): Try and predict when our disks will fail.
  • It was decided that since we are hoping to get some disk-related income soon, we could afford the more expensive enterprise disk.
  • Action (Malcolm): Advise Charles on what to buy.
  • Action (Charles): Buy one 4TB disk (of the aforementioned “very nice” variety), as a spare for when the next disk fails, and have it shipped to Malcolm.
