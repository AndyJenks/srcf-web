Minutes taken by Charles Board

Financial Report:

  • Daniel delivered the Treasurer's report, and a copy, along with a summary of the accounts were circulated.

  • We made a loss in 2009-2010 due to large hardware purchases.

  • So far in the 2010-2011 year, we have made money.

  • We had some problems with the accounts, and so were unable to submit our accounts to the university before the 31st December deadline. As such we may have been deregistered as a society.

  • At the start of term, the committee voted to give Daniel emergency powers to fix the accounts and (if necessary) reregister us.

  • Daniel was able to fix the accounts, and write cheques to cover our debts.

  • A visit to the bank revealed that the signatories on the accounts were incorrect. This has now been fixed.

Sysadmins' Report:

  • This was delivered by Malcolm.

  • There are five new sysadmins: Malte Schwarzkopf, Richard Whitehouse, Ben Millwood, Nick Stenning and Bill Collins.

  • The user-facing servers were upgraded to Ubuntu 10.04.

  • New Services:

    • Usenet server

    • DNSSEC signed zones

    • Less reliance on pip for some services (IRC, DNS), this process will continue

    • Starting to make use of Thunder, the XenServer cluster purchased last year. In particular, Desktop service, Usenet, IRC and DNS. There is still room for more VMs

  • Abuse incidents:

    • On October 27th , one user emitted 120Mbits/s at one address in Trinity Hall. Treated as a DoS attack.

    • The SRCF IRC server was used as the rendezvous for a small botnet.

    • One hacked account was used to host a phishing site. This was taken down, however the RSA request for user data and logs was routinely not complied with.

    • A society hosted a copyright infringing file. It was removed.

    • Some society accounts have been hacked and used to send spam etc. Tends to be caught quickly however.

  • Outages:

    • Pip sometimes goes down, and requires a manual reboot. Generally due to lack of memory. Have attempted running without swap, but this casues other issues.

    • Two of the new servers from last year can't be rebooted without someone physically present to press the switch.

  • Hardware:

    • We have been offered a lot of free hardware by Andrew Moore, although composition is unclear. Suspected to be four full racks, but we only have one rack unit free. We may be able to use some of it to replace old/faulty hardware.

    • Sold an unused rackshelf to retrosnub for ~Â£15

    • Would like to sell Cyclone, Thunder, Locust, Alpha Server and Tape Robot.

  • Projects:

    • Plan to move to subdomain based user websites.

    • Less reliance on pip as it is becoming increasingly unstable with more users and more resource issues. Hope to move user data and shell off pip.
