  • Constitutional Amendment.
    • Old constitution: The Committee shall be elected at the AGM.
    • New constitution: The Committee shall be elected at the AGM by Single Transferable Vote with a RON as defined by the University.

      • For:  20
      • Against: 2
      • Abstain: 3

  • Further constitutional amendments.
    • Amendment 1.
      • Old constitution: Use of the facilities provided by the society shall be restricted to members and subject to an annual fee, which the Committee shall determine, and which may vary according to the type of use made of the facility.
      • New constitution: Use of the facilities provided by the society shall be restricted to members and may be subject to an annual fee, and which the Committee shall determine, and which may vary according to the type of use made of the facility.
      • Reason: Making it clear that fees are optional.
    • Amendment 2.
      • Old constitution: The Committee may also set an annual membership fee of the society, and in addition may offer honorary memberships (free) and life membership (for a fee) to any persons as it sees fit.
      • New constitution: The Committee may also set an annual membership fee of the society, and in addition may offer honorary memberships and life membership to any persons as it sees fit.
      • Reason: Making it clear that fees are optional.
    • Amendment 3.
      • Proposed addition: For official communications of the society email shall be sufficient, and it shall be the responsibility of members of the society to provide the Secretary with an appropriate email address.
      • Reason: To make it clear that we don't have to send letters to people in the post, and that it's not our responsibility to chase people for contact details.
    • Amendments 1-3  were voted on as a single vote as a show of hands.
      • For: 22
      • Against: 1
      • Abstain: 2
      • Amendments 1-3 were therefore accepted.
    • Amendment 4.
      • It suggested that we  adopt the Universities Equal Opportunities Policy and Codes of Practice, but this was rejected.
      • Old constitution: It shall not refuse an application for membership on the grounds of race, sex, sexual orientation, religion, or political views.
      • New constitution: It shall not refuse an application for membership on the grounds of race, sex, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or political views.
      • Reason: Adding disability to the list.
      • A vote on the amendment was held by show of hands.
        • For: 18
        • Against: 3
        • Abstain: 4
      • Amendment 4 was therefore accepted.
    • Amendment 5.
      • Proposed addition: Only one member of the Committee may not be a current University of Cambridge undergraduate or postgraduate student.
      • Reason: The Student-Run Computing Facility must be Student-Run.
      • It was proposed that the SRCF adopt the Junior Proctor's wording on this issue.
      • A vote on the amendment was held by show of hands.
        • Adopt the Junior Proctor's wording: 18
        • Reject proposed changes: 3
        • Abstain: 4
      • Amendment 5 was therefore accepted.
    • Amendment 6.
      • Proposed addition: The society must be registered as a University Society.
      • Reason: So that we can obtain funding from the Societies Syndicate.
      • A vote on the amendment was held by show of hands.
        • For: 5
        • Against: 12
        • Abstain: 8
      • Amendment 6 was therefore rejected.
