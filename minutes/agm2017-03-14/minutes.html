Chair's report (delivered by Charles)

  • "We vote to give this EGM the authority of an AGM and proceed as if it always had been. This expires at the end of the meeting today." - passed unanimously.
  • Firstly, apologies for being slow in organising this AGM.
  • This year is the 18th anniversary of the SRCF.
  • In Easter 2016 we had a successful garden party in the Fellow's Garden at Trinity Hall, and we thank OpenMarket for sponsoring us.
  • The Fellow's Garden was quick to book and worked rather well, suggested for next year's committee.
  • Michaelmas 2016, had the fresher's fair stall with lots of signups and lots of interest
  • Stuck with pen and paper approach, though a few people did comment on the low tech nature
  • Was suggested that we might move to a system where people scan their CamCards if someone bothers to implement this
  • Proposal: Invest in newer publicity material
  • Squash happened, many people showed up and snacks were provided.
  • Lent 2017, our main event was the 18th anniversary dinner, generously supported by Third Light Ltd. (who would be interested in people who are good at JavaScript/Go to contact them for summer internship opportunities)
  • Membership stands at 3372 users, 9050 members and 1870 societies
  • Growth in users probably partially due to no longer cancelling accounts automatically; but still appears to be a healthy number of users
  • In terms of future, hardware from Third Light offered, need to investigate their decomissioned servers
  • Still working on upgrade of Ubuntu to 16.04
  • Stash: Tentative plans with Tejas, not arranged yet, to be arranged by new committee.
  • Thanks to the outgoing committee, sysadmins and all of our members

Sysadmin's Report (delivered by Richard)

  • Control panel - making good progress, working, basically ready, need to test more and rebuild when we move to sinkhole
  • sinkhole - moving srcf to new web server that's not pip, going pretty well I suppose. Reason why it's taking so long, ubuntu 12.04 and 16.04, lots of changes to apache and mysql, a lot of which break compatibility with the 1000s of websites on srcf. Move to apache 2.4 likely to break much, move to mysql has already broken much
  • Unavoidably disruptive, aiming to move over before 12.04 moves out of security support in April.
  • Will ask societies to test their sites before we flip the switch
  • Regular if not frequent hackdays, we need more sysadmins
  • Vast majority of development work seem to happen during hackdays, need to organise more of them. At least 1/2 times a term
