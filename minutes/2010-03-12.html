Domain names

  • We now own srcf.net and srcf.co.uk. Julian Midgley has obtained srcf.info on our behalf

  • We want to move to using srcf.net, and have srcf.co.uk, srcf.ucam.org and possibly srcf.info redirect to srcf.net.

Move to sub-domains

  • $CRSid.{me,user,users}.srcf.net and $SocName.{soc,socs,societies}.srcf.net

  • Everyone wants sub-domains there are various technical and aesthetic reasons for this.

  • We can get wild-card SSL certificates from quakenet for free.

  • There may be other companies that do free wild-card certificates...

  • The problem would be that if we commit to having 3 wild-card certificates forever. This could be quite expensive if we can't get them for free any more.

  • We could have *.srcf.net but there are issues with that.

    • Sysadmins don't want a wild-card DNS at *.srcf.net and so a large DNS zone would be required.

      • There is a good article on why this is so [http://www.iab.org/documents/docs/2003-09-20-dns-wildcards.html].

    • DPA issues (raised by the UCS) can (probably) be sorted with disallowing zone transfers

    • Issues with caching the non existance of DNS records resulting in people not being able to access their site for several hours after signing up.

    • Action: we will use srcf.net

      • This should happen over the summer.

      • Sysadmins should discuss and agree on how to do sub-domains soon (before the end of the Easter holiday).

SRCF CA PGP key

  • This is a plan of Daniel Thomas to create a SRCF CA (Certificate Authority) PGP key and store it on a medium not connected to anything, on an encrypted volume and preferably physically locked up.

  • This would in turn trust a signing key which would be used to sign and trust the secretary and chairman's etc. PGP keys and the public SSH and SSL keys to provide a web of trust.
