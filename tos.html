Terms of Service

The SRCF has updated its terms of service to better reflect the current state of affairs.

In summary, we have:

  • added formal definitions for members and users of the SRCF
  • replaced use of the term society with the more general group, in line with changes made elsewhere on our website
  • updated links to external rules provided by UIS and JANET
  • removed the requirement for users to be authorised users of the CUDN in their own right
  • added the requirement that users must remain contactable in order to continue using their accounts
  • defined how we handle data of unmaintained accounts

New members registered on or after Friday 19 March 2021 will be subject to version 2 of the terms.

Existing members with dormant users accounts will switch to version 2 of the terms upon reactivation, or on Monday 19 April 2021, whichever comes first.

For existing users with active accounts, by continuing to use our services on or after Monday 19 April 2021, you agree to these changes. You may alternatively contact the sysadmins to request the termination of your membership (and user account if you have one).

Definitions

Any reference in these terms to "we" or "us" refers to the SRCF System Administrators as appointed by the SRCF Committee.

Any Member whose personal SRCF user account ("Personal account") is enabled at a given time is also a "User" during that time. Some SRCF services are available to Users only, more broadly to Members, or to the wider University community or to the public.

Contact requirements

  1. We must be able to contact all Users. If we can't contact you (for example, if our emails to you bounce, or if you do not reply to an email within a reasonable time), your Personal account and your access to services as a User will be forfeit. It is your responsibility to ensure that we have an up-to-date email address for you and that you promptly respond to requests from the committee or sysadmins.

Group accounts

  1. Group accounts must have one or more active Users registered as Group Admins. If a User's Personal account is cancelled, they will also be removed from any Groups for which they are an Admin. If a Group no longer has any registered Admins, the Group account itself will also become suspended (and later deleted; see 10). It is the responsibility of a Group's Admins to maintain an up-to-date roster of Admins.

  2. Admins of a Group account share joint responsibility for the upkeep, contents and actions of that account.

  3. Personal accounts must not be used to host anything which might need to be maintained by more than one person, such as a society website; you should apply for a Group account for this purpose.

Data retention and security

Please see our privacy statement for more information about how we handle your data.

  1. You grant us permission to view any information stored, processed, transmitted or received by our systems for the purposes of ensuring correct operation, integrity, security and compliance with these Terms of Service and relevant legislation.

  2. You are not permitted to compromise or allow to be compromised the security, privacy, integrity or availability of another person's or Group's account or data, or of the SRCF itself. In particular you may not access another person's data without their explicit consent.

  3. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your account(s). This means, for example, that:

    1. no password may be easily guessable;
    2. you must not share any password or other security credential;
    3. you must not give anybody else privileged access to your account(s), other than by making them a Group Admin;
    4. you must endeavour to update any software installed in your account(s), particularly when an updated version fixing a security vulnerability is released.

  4. You must take full responsibility for dealing with the consequences you suffer from data loss or disruption to SRCF services. The SRCF is not in a position to make any guarantees whatsoever. So, for example, you must maintain your own backups of any irreplaceable data you store on the SRCF. We strongly recommend that you do not use the SRCF as part of any safety-critical system.

  5. We will delete all data stored in a Personal or Group account if the account remains cancelled or suspended for an extended period of time.

  6. You may not wilfully or recklessly waste or cause to be wasted the resources or time of the SRCF, its volunteers or its providers.

Changes

  1. We may make changes to these terms in future. If we do so, we will notify all people to whom the changes apply at least 30 days before the new terms come into effect.
